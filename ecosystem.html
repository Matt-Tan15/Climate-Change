The damage of climate change to the ecosystems in the arctic has perhaps been the most well noted. Scientists speculate that arctic animals that are reliant on the ice and cold temperatures such as the iconic polar bear won’t be able to adapt to rising temperatures and may become extinct.
The ecosystems in the Amazon Rainforest are at risk of both droughts, and excessive flooding due to the continued clearing of forests, illegal forest fires, agriculture and mining. These activities caused by human hands hinders the capacity at which the forest can take up carbon.
Due to sea level rise, beaches will be impacted right away with coastal ecosystems in danger of flooding, and heat waves causing harmful algae blooms, among other impacts.
